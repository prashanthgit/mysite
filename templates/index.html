Phase transition of Quarks and Gluons

When two nucleus collide nearly at the speed of the light in the collider, plasma of quarks and gluons formed for a very short time before the plasma converted into thousands of ordinary particles. To improve the statistical precision, billions of such nuclear collisions get recorded each year, which can easily take petabytes of data. My work is to study the flow of the emitted particles to understand the phase transition of quarks and gluons. I have cleaned up about 3PB of data using hundreds of Linux cores to isolate rare particles, and applied regression techniques on them to look for correlations among different observables. My study showed a new correlation between the centrality of nuclear collision and the flow of the particles. Journal article News letter
